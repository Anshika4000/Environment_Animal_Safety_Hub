Improvement Tips

Expert recommendations to optimize your compost quality

High Priority

Balance Your Carbon:Nitrogen Ratio

Maintain a C:N ratio between 25:1 and 30:1 for optimal microbial activity. Add more carbon-rich materials like dry leaves or straw if your ratio is too low.

Medium Priority

Monitor Moisture Levels

Keep moisture content between 50-60% for best results. Squeeze a handful of compost - it should feel like a wrung-out sponge. Add water if too dry, more carbon if too wet.

Medium Priority

Ensure Proper Aeration

Turn your compost pile every 1-2 weeks to provide oxygen for aerobic bacteria. Poor aeration leads to anaerobic decomposition and unpleasant odors.

Low Priority

Avoid Problem Materials

Don't add meat, dairy, oils, or pet waste to your compost pile. These materials can attract pests and create odors. Stick to plant-based materials only.

High Priority

Maintain Pile Size

Keep your compost pile at least 3 feet x 3 feet x 3 feet for proper heat retention. Smaller piles cool down too quickly and decomposition slows significantly.

Low Priority

Layer Materials Properly

Alternate layers of carbon-rich (brown) and nitrogen-rich (green) materials. This creates air pockets and provides a balanced food source for microorganisms.
