About

This platform helps households and communities identify compost contamination sources (plastic bits, chemicals, wrong waste mixing) and improve compost quality workflows. Better compost means healthier soil and more trust in urban sustainability efforts.

Common Contamination Sources

  • Plastic bits: Packaging, wrappers, bags
  • Chemicals: Pesticide residues, cleaning agents
  • Wrong waste: Meat, dairy, cooked food, glass, metals
  • Non-biodegradable items: Rubber, synthetic fibers

Detection & Testing

  • Visual inspection for plastics, glass, and metals
  • Smell test for chemical residues
  • Sieving to remove small non-organics
  • pH and moisture testing kits
  • Community lab testing (optional)

Compost Quality Workflows

  1. Segregate waste at source (kitchen, garden, others)
  2. Educate all household members on what goes in compost
  3. Use color-coded bins for easy sorting
  4. Regularly inspect and remove contaminants
  5. Turn and aerate compost weekly
  6. Test compost before use

Improvement Tips

  • Host composting workshops in your community
  • Share success stories and best practices
  • Set up a neighborhood compost quality team
  • Use digital tools to track contamination incidents
  • Reward households with best compost quality

FAQ

Q: Why is plastic in compost a problem?
A: Plastics do not break down and can harm soil and plants.

Q: How can I test for chemicals?
A: Use smell, pH kits, or community labs.

Q: What should never go in compost?
A: Meat, dairy, glass, metals, synthetic materials.

Q: How can I improve my compost quality?
A: Follow the workflows and tips above!
