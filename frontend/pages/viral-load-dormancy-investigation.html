Increased Viral Load During Winter Dormancy

metabolic immunology · low‑temperature dynamics

Description

Investigate immune suppression thresholds in low‑temperature metabolic states. Study how viral load dynamics change during winter dormancy periods in relevant organisms — from amphibians to mammalian hibernators.

Problem statement

During winter dormancy, metabolic rate drops substantially, often accompanied by immune modulation. Suppressed immunity may allow enhanced viral replication and prolonged persistence. Understanding this threshold is critical for predicting seasonal outbreaks and designing interventions.

Proposed investigation

  • Measure viral loads in dormant hosts under controlled hypothermic conditions (4 °C → 10 °C).
  • Quantify immune markers (cytokines, toll‑like receptors) to pinpoint suppression thresholds.
  • Compare with active metabolic states to isolate dormancy‑specific effects.

Expected outcome

  • Identify temperature–metabolism thresholds where viral proliferation accelerates.
  • Provide insights into host–pathogen dynamics during hibernation / winter dormancy.
  • Suggest intervention windows (e.g., immune adjuvants before dormancy).
